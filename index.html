Welcome to Threshold's Online Auralization Studio where you can hear your voice as a performer inside some of the world's best rooms.
We recommend grabbing your wired headphones and using Chrome for the best experience. Tablets and phones are not supported.
Use the buttons and pop up menus to record your voice, listen to recordings, and travel between rooms.

Brockman Hall for Opera

Lucian and Nancy Morrison Theater

Configured for Opera

An educational facility, the Opera Performance Hall at the Shepherd School of Music, has an acoustic character that stretches and supports the next generation of professional opera singers. The 600-seat theatre’s design originated from visits and research undertaken by the school and design team to benchmark facilities like the Royal Opera at the Palace of Versailles and Estates Theatre in Prague.

Owner: Rice University
Architect: Allan Greenberg Architect
Theatre Consultant: Fisher Dachs Associates
Acoustician: Threshold Acoustics

For more information visit Threshold Acoustics

Writers' Theatre

The intimacy found in 250-seat Nichols Theater is created in part by the back screen made of brick salvaged from the original building, transforming as it rises to an open, variegated sculpture. The screen speaks softly back to the actors, creating acoustic intimacy for performers and audiences.

Owner: Writers' Theatre
Architect: Studio Gang Architects
Theatre Consultant: Auerbach Pollock Friedlander
Acoustician: Threshold Acoustics

For more information visit Threshold Acoustics

Brown Performing Arts Center

Recital

A theatre, an experimental media room, and a recital hall. Brown University’s Performing Arts Center provides room for all three within a small footprint by moving the walls and ceiling to fit the performances’ needs. Varying the intimacy and absorption of the room itself keeps the audience close to the action, no matter the situation.

Owner: Brown Arts Initiative
Architect: REX
Theatre Consultant: Theatre Consultant
Acoustician: Threshold Acoustics

For more information visit Threshold Acoustics
